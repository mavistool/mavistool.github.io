MAVIS: An Intelligent Multimodal Audio-Visual Interactive System Enabling Fluid, Continuous Dialogues

Abstract

With the rapid advancement of human-computer interaction technologies, interactive companion models for voice dialogue are gaining considerable attention. As a result, developing a model that can interpret real-world audio-visual information and engage in multi-turn spoken dialogue presents great potential to enhance user experience. To this end, we propose MAVIS, a model trained on comprehensive synthetic data that utilizes cross-modal attention mechanisms to effectively interpret audio-visual information from video scenes. By drawing on this modality knowledge, MAVIS generates high-quality dialogue responses that ensure both contextual relevance and stylistic consistency. Evaluations across multiple open-source QA benchmarks underscore MAVIS’s robust multimodal understanding abilities. Furthermore, both subjective and objective metrics confirm its ability to generate contextually appropriate and stylistically consistent outputs in multi-turn dialogue. Our dataset and code will soon be released at www.mavistool.github.io in the future.
